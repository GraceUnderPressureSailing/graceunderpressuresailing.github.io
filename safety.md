# Safety and limitations

These pages describe personal projects installed and tested on one particular
vessel. They are shared for information, discussion and inspiration—not as
professional marine engineering, electrical or navigational advice.

Equipment, installations and regulatory requirements differ. Verify all work
against current manufacturer instructions and the requirements applicable to
your vessel. Use a suitably qualified professional where appropriate. No
warranty is given for the accuracy, completeness or fitness of any material on
this site.

## Electrical and machinery work

Marine batteries can deliver destructive currents, and machinery may move
without warning. Use suitable isolation, circuit protection, test procedures
and personal protective equipment. A diagram showing one installation does not
establish suitable conductor sizes, protection or switching for another.

## Navigation and passage material

Tracks, maps, diagrams, instrument data and passage notes are historical or
illustrative. They are not navigation charts, current pilotage directions or a
substitute for official publications, Notices to Mariners, weather, tides,
local instructions or proper watchkeeping.

## Software and recorded data

Software may contain defects and recorded data may be incomplete, delayed or
incorrect. Supplemental projects must be arranged so that their failure cannot
impair the vessel's essential equipment.
