# Bow-thruster control replacement

**Status:** Installed; detailed write-up in preparation

This project replaced an ageing bow-thruster control arrangement aboard
*Grace Under Pressure*. The eventual case study will cover:

- the symptoms and investigation of the original system;
- the requirements placed on a replacement;
- the control architecture and retained safety functions;
- electrical isolation and unintended-operation risks;
- bench, dockside and operational commissioning; and
- faults, limitations and lessons from use.

The objective of the write-up is to document the reasoning and tests, not to
offer a universal wiring recipe. Cable protection, switching, interlocks and
manufacturer requirements are specific to the installed equipment and vessel.

> **Safety-critical system:** A bow thruster combines high-current electrical
> equipment with machinery capable of moving unexpectedly. Isolate all relevant
> supplies and obtain competent marine-electrical assistance where appropriate.
> Do not reproduce this vessel's eventual design without an independent safety
> assessment.
